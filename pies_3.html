Rhubarb Apple Pie

Ingredients:

2 unbaked pie crusts (homemade or store-bought)
3 cups thinly sliced and peeled apples (use a mixture of sweet and tart apples)
3 cups chopped rhubarb
3/4 cup granulated sugar
1/4 cup all-purpose flour
1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
1/4 teaspoon salt
2 tablespoons unsalted butter, cut into small pieces
1 egg, beaten
Coarse sugar for sprinkling on top (optional)

Preheat your oven to 400°F (205°C).
In a large bowl, mix together the sliced apples, chopped rhubarb, sugar, flour, cinnamon, nutmeg, and salt until well combined.
Place one pie crust into a 9-inch pie dish and gently press it into the bottom and up the sides of the dish.
Pour the apple and rhubarb mixture into the pie crust, making sure to distribute the fruit evenly.
Dot the top of the fruit with small pieces of butter.
Place the second pie crust over the fruit and crimp the edges of the two crusts together.
Use a sharp knife to cut a few slits in the top of the pie crust to allow steam to escape.
Brush the top of the crust with the beaten egg and sprinkle with coarse sugar (if using).
Bake the pie for 15 minutes at 400°F (205°C), then reduce the oven temperature to 350°F (175°C) and continue baking for another 45-50 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the filling is bubbling.
Allow the pie to cool completely before slicing and serving.
Enjoy your delicious rhubarb and apple pie, which has a nice balance of sweetness and tartness from the apples and rhubarb!
